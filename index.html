Hi there! I'm Peter Flanagan, and I am computer science major at the University of Michigan. I was born in rainy Seattle Washington, and moved around until the third grade when I found my home in the suburbs of Philadelphia. I began programming in middle school when my computer applications teacher told me I made a decent looking website. That year I submitted a small website on Fractal Geometry to the PA State Computer Fair. I won second place at the state finals, and was instantly trust into a world of wanting to know more.

Since then I've improved my web designing skills, done graphic design for my student organization, and have expanded my programming knowledge into other areas.

Colors Of Pi

This one is another art project that depicts the hexadecimal colors that pi makes when you split it into sixes. For example: 3.14159 becomes #314159, which is a shade of blue. If you are nerdy and like Pi, I would suggest checking it out. It also looks great as a desktop background if I do say so myself.
